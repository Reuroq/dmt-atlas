What this is — and what it isn't

This is a map of what people report and write about the DMT experience, synthesized from published books, peer-reviewed studies, and community archives. Every entry is cited. It is a cartography of a shared phenomenology and mythology — not a claim that these are literally real places or beings, and not a guide to obtaining, making, or taking any substance.

  • Map of reports, not metaphysics. Recurring entities/realms are what strangers consistently describe — that the descriptions converge is the interesting, documented fact; it is not proof of an external dimension.
  • The famous image is not the common one. 'Machine elves' are iconic (McKenna) but a minority percept (~2.9% of one report set; ~10% by one researcher's estimate). The single most-reported figure is a feminine/Goddess presence (~24%).
  • Numbers come only from named studies. Where a frequency is given, the study is named. 'More real than real' is felt near-universally but stated in those exact words by only ~0.6% (Lawrence 2022) — the feeling is pervasive, the literal phrase rare.
  • The journey order is a common scaffold, not a law. Every account differs; the waiting-room↔breakthrough line is largely dose-driven. Many people skip stages or land straight in the void.
  • It is not all love and safety. The benevolent majority (~78%) coexists with a fear/menace minority (~8% malicious; 41% felt fear at some point) and a 'you're not supposed to be here' current.
  • Some labels are cultural imports. 'Reptilian' and 'Grey' map onto pre-existing conspiracy/UFO scripts; researchers note the brain reliably foregrounds an agent, but 'the specific make-up of the agent is culturally determined' (Timmermann).
  • Source tiers: peer-reviewed surveys/field studies (Davis 2020, Lawrence 2022, Michael 2021, Timmermann 2018/2019) > clinical narrative (Strassman) & cross-substance scholarship (Shanon) > community lexicon (DMT-Nexus) & popular syntheses. McKenna is one vivid primary account, not a representative sample.

The Teacher — professor of hyperspace

Also called: The Professor, The Instructor, The Guide-Teacher, Diplomatic anthropologists (McKenna)

teacherPhase 7
How often is this reported?
One of the best-attested roles: 'guide' was the single most-selected descriptor in the Johns Hopkins survey (43% of 2,561 respondents); Michael et al. (2021) found 'showing or communing' behavior in 47% of encounters and teaching/guidance messages in multiple cases; Meyer's corpus lists teaching/instruction among the most common entity activities.

✦Appearance

Highly variable but consistently authoritative: a robed elder, a scholarly humanoid, a formless voice-with-presence, or an ordinary-seeming person radiating superhuman intelligence. Often appears singly, positioned as if the experiencer has entered a classroom, lecture, or tutorial. The DMT-Nexus lexicon records long-running relationships with a consistent 'Guide' personality across many sessions.

✦Behavior

Deliberate demonstration and instruction: showing objects, diagrams, or scenes; walking the experiencer through lessons; correcting misunderstandings; assigning what feel like exercises or homework. Teachers are patient but businesslike, and reports emphasize the sense of a curriculum — that the lesson was prepared and the student expected. Meyer's corpus documents entities demonstrating techniques (e.g., breathing, yoga-like postures) and symbolic knowledge.

✦Communication

The most linguistically rich of entity types: fluent telepathy, direct 'knowledge transfer' where understanding arrives whole, visual demonstration with commentary, occasionally heard speech. McKenna characterized his teachers as trying to transmit a new language.

✦Emotional tone

Benevolent, patient, occasionally stern or disappointed; experiencers often report feeling like a small child or new student before a vast intelligence.

✦Message or purpose

Instruction: about death, love, the structure of reality, how to live, or skills to practice. The felt purpose is the experiencer's development — many report the same teacher resuming lessons across separate sessions.

✦What the sources say

'Guide' was the most frequently selected entity descriptor (43%) among 2,561 survey respondents describing their single most memorable DMT entity encounter.
— Davis, Clifton, Weaver, Hurwitz, Johnson & Griffiths (Johns Hopkins) — Survey of entity encounter experiences occasioned by inhaled N,N-DMT (2020)
Described spirit-guide 'teachers' intent on transmitting language and knowledge; St. John summarizes McKenna's framing of them as 'diplomatic anthropologists... coming to give us the keys to galactic citizenship'.
— Terence McKenna — True Hallucinations / The Archaic Revival / lectures (1991–1993)
Mystery School in Hyperspace documents the teacher/mystery-school framing across decades of DMT culture, including reports of councils and teachers imparting knowledge, laws, and instructions.
— Graham St John — The Breakthrough Experience: DMT hyperspace and its liminal aesthetics (2018)
Lexicon contributor NGC_2264 describes 'The Guide' — 'a calm, gentle, benevolent teacher... responsible for teaching me many lessons which seemed to be improving my life' — recurring across sessions.
— DMT-Nexus community — The Hyperspace Lexicon (n.d.)
Corpus includes entities giving instruction — demonstrating techniques and symbolic knowledge — as a common activity class.
— Peter Meyer — 340 DMT Trip Reports (and 'Concerning the Nature of the DMT Entities') (2005)
'Showing or communing' (presenter/focuser role) coded in 47% of encounters; explicit teaching/guidance messages recorded in multiple cases.
— Michael, Luke & Robinson — An Encounter With the Other: a thematic & content analysis of DMT experiences from a naturalistic field study (2021)

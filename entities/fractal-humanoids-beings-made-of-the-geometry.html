Fractal humanoids — beings made of the geometry

Also called: Geometric beings, Pattern-people, Fractal elves, Beings of living mathematics

impersonal-intelligencePhase 4
How often is this reported?
Michael et al. (2021) coded 'sentient structures' in 25% of encounters — the nearest studied category; Effect Index documents geometry-composed entities as one of its two compositional classes. A standing description in community archives ('the pattern became a person'). Uncounted as its own survey category.

✦Appearance

Humanoid silhouettes constituted entirely of the surrounding visual geometry: bodies of tessellating pattern, filigree, lattice, or recursive fractal structure, continuous with — and often emerging out of — the pattern-field around them. Effect Index explicitly notes entities may be 'composed of either geometric structures or realistic materials'; these are the geometric pole. Their outlines hold while their substance perpetually self-transforms, kaleidoscopic and boundaryless at the edges.

✦Behavior

Emergence, display, and reabsorption: they condense from the pattern, interact — often by modulating the very geometry they are made of, sending waves of structure toward the experiencer — and dissolve back. Frequently plural and synchronized, moving as if the pattern itself had decided to be several people for a while. Michael et al.'s 'sentient structures' coding (25% of encounters) captures the adjacent phenomenon of environment-as-being.

✦Communication

Geometry as language: transmissions arrive as pattern-changes, unfolding forms, and structured light experienced as meaningful — closer to being shown mathematics than being spoken to.

✦Emotional tone

Impersonal but not cold; wonder, alien intelligence, and the vertiginous sense that the distinction between place, pattern, and person has failed.

✦Message or purpose

Often interpreted as the space itself taking a face to interact — the geometry's ambassadors. Content is typically structural insight (how the space works) rather than personal guidance.

— Terence McKenna — True Hallucinations / The Archaic Revival / lectures (1991–1993)
